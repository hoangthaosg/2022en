1. Money

1. What do people use to make purchases in your country?
In my country most people use credit and debit cards to make purchases. However, most people also carry cash and loose change in order to buy smaller items. Checks are sometimes used to pay bills.
2. Are coins or bills more common in your country? Which do you prefer?
Bills are more common in my country than coins. I prefer them because they are not heavy and they can be used to pay for almost anything. Coins can only be used to purchase small items because it is difficult to carry large amounts of them around, and most shops will not allow you to buy expensive items with them.

2. Bank supplies

1. What supplies does a bank teller need?
A bank teller needs a variety of supplies, including pens, stamps and ink, a cash drawer and holders for bills and coins.
2. What does your bank do when supplies run low?
When my bank runs low on supplies it orders new ones from our supplier.

3. Bank furniture

1. What furniture items do you find in a bank?
Items of furniture that you find in a bank include chairs and desks for the staff, couches and chairs for customers, and conference tables to allow business meetings to take place.
2. If you were a bank manager, how would you furnish your bank?
If I were a bank manager I would furnish my bank with modern furniture as well as more classic items like a mahogany or oak desk.

4. Bank documents

1. What documents are required to open a bank account in your country?
To open a bank account in my country you need a Social Security Number (SSN), an acceptable form of identification with your picture on it (such as a passport or driving license), and some proof of where you live such as a utility bill (e.g. water or electricity).
2. What documents does a teller use in transactions?
Tellers use a variety of documents in transactions. Withdrawal forms are used when customers withdraw money, account application forms are used when someone wishes to open a new account, change of address forms are used when a customer wishes to notify a bank that they are moving house and so on.

5. Bank machines

1. What kinds of machines are used in banks?
Banks use a variety of machines to make them more efficient and secure. Currency counters are used when customers make withdrawals or deposits and counterfeit detectors are used to check that bills presented are genuine. Various other standard pieces of office equipment are also used, for example computers and printers.
2. What security measures do banks take in your country?
Banks employ a large number of security measures in my country. Security guards and cameras are always present and tellers are protected by bullet-proof screens. Paper shredders are also used to protect sensitive customer and bank information.
